History of astronomy

  1. Ancient civilizations made significant early contributions to astronomy. Babylonians recorded celestial events; Egyptians aligned their architectural structures with celestial bodies.
  2. Greek philosophers developed the geocentric model, placing Earth at the center of the universe.
  3. The Ptolemaic system described the universe as concentric spheres with Earth at the center.
  4. Nicolaus Copernicus introduced the heliocentric model, positioning the Sun at the center of the solar system.
  5. Galileo Galilei observed celestial phenomena such as the moons of Jupiter and the phases of Venus, supporting the heliocentric model.
  6. Johannes Kepler formulated laws of planetary motion, showing planets move in elliptical orbits.
  7. Isaac Newton's work on universal gravitation provided a comprehensive explanation of celestial forces and laid the groundwork for classical mechanics.
  8. Edwin Hubble's observations led to the discovery that the universe is expanding, supporting the Big Bang theory.
  9. The discovery of cosmic microwave background radiation in 1965 provided further evidence for the Big Bang theory and enhanced our understanding of the universe's origins.
  10. Modern astronomy advances with innovations in space telescopes, radio astronomy, and deep-space exploration, expanding our knowledge of the cosmos.
